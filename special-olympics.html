PROJECT OVERVIEW

What is Special Olympics?

The Special Olympics is the world’s largest humanitarian sporting event and a global movement to empower people with intellectual disabilities through sport. Special Olympics of North Macedonia is a non-profit organization recognized by the International Special Olympics as a member.

We were given the task to create an official and professional modernized website for Special Olympics North Macedonia, where they can present their work, attract more partners, volunteers and donors.
This project was assigned to us after just four months into the bootcamp and it was the first real project where we worked with real clients and proposed a real solution.

PROBLEM

The lack of an official website contributed to lack of interest in the public to donate, partners for long-term cooperation and impractical ways of motivating and recruiting volunteers

The Special Olympics of North Macedonia, even though it is a very active organization, with plenty of members and achievements both on a national and international level, did not have an official website where they can inform the public and members about ongoing activities and the noble work they are doing. Instead, the organization has relied on facebook and traditional media such as television and radio to share its message.

GOAL

Increase donations, recruit volunteers and attract partners

Our goal for this project was to create an official website that will be a transparent platform showcasing work, achievements, projects and their impact, in order to prompt more partnerships and donations. Website that will act as a hub and information center for people with intellectual disabilities, families, friends and a wider range of users interested in inclusive sport related content.

DESIGN PROCESS

Double Diamond

With the team we followed Double Diamond as a process. We implemented different research methods, collected and analysed data, and always questioned, reiterated and re-evaluated our design based on the needs of our target users.

STAKEHOLDER INTERVIEW

The purpose of the website is not to target people with intellectual disabilities

At the beginning of the project, we were focused on collecting ideas, requirements and business goals that are directly related to the project. We had a Zoom meeting and interviewed three stakeholders.

During the stakeholder interview, we learned that the purpose of the website is not to target people with intellectual disabilities, instead we had to focus on representing their stories and achievements. The website is going to be a starting point for communication with their current partners and digital exposure to the public.

RESEARCH

Competitive Analysis

To gain inspiration and identify best practices for our website, we conducted a competitive analysis. We began by looking at the websites of several Special Olympics including SO General, SO Sweden, SO Washington, SO France and SO UK.

One common feature we identified across these websites was the placement of the donate button in order to emphasize it as the main call to action. This was an important design pattern to consider in order to make it easier and more efficient for users to get involved and donate.

Another common design pattern we observed was that each non-profit prominently displayed their mission statement at the beginning of their homepage. This was an important method to effectively communicate nonprofit's mission to the user and help them better understand what the organization does.

On the donate page, many non-profits had pre-determined donation amounts in order to make the donation process easier and more efficient for users.

USER INTERVIEW

Understanding good intentions

Donor

I’ve conducted User Interviews to get a better understanding of the users’ needs and goals. I interviewed five participants via Zoom. While talking to the research participants about their donating habits, I was surprised to find that the primary considerations for donors were:

Transparency:  people wanted to know exactly where their money donation were going

Making an impact:  knowing their donation was having an impact

Trust and credibility:  feeling confidence in the organization was a major precursor to donate to that organization as well as empathy and urge to help.

Volunteers

In order to understand what makes people decide which nonprofit organization to be involved the key insights were:

Findability of information  is critical for people to make decisions on whether to get involved.

People want  to see impact and results  before getting involved.

IDEATE

User flow

User flow is the path taken by the user on a website to complete a task. The user flow takes them from their entry point through a set of steps towards a successful outcome and final action such as making a donation or register to the organization like volunteer or partner. Scenario: User making a donation

IDEATE

Information architecture

Upon setting up the product goals, user needs and deciding what features to include, we continue building up the information architecture. We organized, structured and labeled content in an effective and intuitive way so users can easily navigate the site and find information without much effort.

Users will have access to information through the navigation menu, which is divided into six categories: About Us, Get involved, Sports, Programs, Multimedia and Contact.

DESIGN

Explore different ideas before settling on a direction

Before diving into the designs, I started with low-fidelity sketches. This technique enables me to move fast and explore different ideas before settling on a direction. I sketched out most of the pages but the most challenging part was to nail the donation page.


Why was this a challenge?

I had to present the bank account of the organization and let the users know how to donate using a payment slip. According to the research we conducted, 5 out of 10 people still make mistakes or are confused when filling in a payment slip.
Therefore, in our design I have included an example of how to fill in a payment slip, so it will be easier for the users to make a donation.

Donation page prototype

Balancing incentives and controls with trust and credibility being central pillars of our product, we wanted to encourage charitable donations only as much as users want. This way they didn't feel pressured to donate more than what they were comfortable with.

  • On the donate page users can read how they can make donation and where the donation is going.
  • Users in North Macedonia can donate with payment slip and can see example how to fill in a payment slip.
  • Users can set on how much they donate, or they can choose from pre-determined donation amounts in order to make the donation process easier and more efficient.

Final designs

When moving to high fidelity, the biggest challenge was designing for mobile and desktop viewports whilst still keeping consistency. I wanted to keep the designs simple and clear, but also give information about the non-profit organization to engage the user immediately.

Our design is based on emotion, visually representing the happiness, smiles and gratitude of the athletes. In order to attract more donations, we represented the success of the athletes, rather than their dependence, so that our design would evoke an urge for support rather than pityness.

Home page

The home page is designed to communicate the goals and values of the organization and find people that would support and get involved in helping to achieve them.

I aimed to ensure that the content was engaging, and the call to action (to donate) was clear.

1. Mission statement a clear formulated goal that introduces the organisation even to first time users

2. News sectionwhere family and friends of people with intellectual disability can be informed for everything SPecial Olympics related

3. Call-to-action buttons to donate were strategically placed on the main navigation and in between the homepage to reinforce the donation and make donating easier for the user

4. Banner for inviting partners and volunteers to support the organization

5. Support athlete is section where we want to introduce the athletes and their achievements in order to establish an emotional connection with the users, which is crucial for increasing donations

About us

Users wanted to feel trust and credibility in where their donation were going, and transparency in both the organization and how funds were being used was of top importance. With this in mind, we designed the website with transparency at the forefront.

In about us page users can read important information about annual plans and reports, revenue and expenses, the mission statement and the company history. With this information users can know exactly where, how and when their money is being put to good use.

Sports

The sport page has information for all the sports offered by the organization. The user (especially families and friends of athletes) will be able to read more about the sports, the date and location where trainings are held and a short biography of the coaches.

Get involved

Based on our research, for volunteers and partners it was important findability of information and easy registration.

To improve findability we focused on writing good content that is clear and concise.

For the registration we used familiar user interface pattern, the registration form is familiar layout and format for users.

CONCLUSIONS

Lessons learned

When this project was given to us, we were at the beginning of our UX Academy, and we had never met each other. It was a great learning experience to apply our new UX skills, whilst working as a team. We all had very different backgrounds and skills to bring to the table. At first, it took a while for us to adjust to each other’s working styles. However, halfway through the project we did a retrospective session, and I was appointed as a team lead. As a team lead I ensured that everyone had opportunity to share their idea with a focus to our project to move forward.

There was time when a teammate and I had ideas that were so different that we wouldn't be able to realistically accommodate both in our design. Having to make a decision to choose between one or the other was hard. At the end, what I learned is not which design is selected or which designer is correct. What matters is which design will lead to a better experience for the user.

I found it really valuable to have a retrospective, to have that open and honest conversation of where we were doing great and where we could improve. These steps made a huge difference, and we worked as a productive, efficient and happy team.
